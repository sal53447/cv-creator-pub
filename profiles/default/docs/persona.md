# Persona — {{YOUR NAME}}

*This file captures beliefs, values, voice, and working style — the stuff that doesn't live in the CV. Skills and agents (cv-talent-advisor, cover-letter-coach) should read this as the primary source for tone and positioning decisions. First-person throughout.*

---

**How to fill this in:** This is a template. Each section below has a prompt. Replace the prompt with your own answer in your own voice. Keep the sections that apply to you; delete or replace any that don't. The skills and agents will read whatever is here, so write as if you're speaking directly to a reader — not as if you're filling in a form.

If a section doesn't apply to you, delete the section entirely. An overlay at `profiles/{slug}/docs/persona.md` will only add or override sections this file doesn't already cover, so it pays to be specific here rather than generic.

---

## 1. How I think about [your field]

Write your core conviction about how your work should be done. The opinions you'd defend in an argument. What's your take on the hype vs. reality of the current state of your field? What do you believe most people get wrong?

*Prompt example:* "AI is just a tool. The real story of software engineering is..."

---

## 2. Red lines

Two to four things you will not work around. Specific enough that you'd actually walk from a job over them — not generic "I value integrity" statements.

*Prompt examples:* team cultures you can't function in, industries/products you won't build for, working styles that drain you past productive output.

---

## 3. Values I operate from

The actual values that shape your day-to-day decisions. Avoid corporate-poster values ("integrity", "excellence"). Use "X over Y" pairs to reveal real tradeoffs.

*Prompt examples:* "clarity over politics", "enabling others over hoarding expertise", "shipping over polish".

---

## 4. What energizes me / what drains me

Specific conditions that make you produce your best work, and specific dynamics that drain you. Not "I like challenge" — things like "teaching non-technical teams" or "stagnant feedback loops."

## 5. Voice & tone

How you sound when you're being yourself. Direct vs. diplomatic? Dry vs. earnest? Technical precision vs. plain-speaking? If possible, paste 2–3 short samples of your own writing (Slack messages, emails, docs) that sound like you — an agent can mirror them when drafting cover letters.

---

## 6. Narrative thread

The story running under your career that ties disparate roles together. You probably have one or two. Cover-letter agents use this as the thread that keeps the letter from reading as assembled from scraps.

---

## 7. Self-awareness

What you know you're not, or are still growing into. Written honestly, not as false modesty. Prevents agents from overclaiming on your behalf — which gets caught in interviews.

---

## 8. Career direction & why

Where you're trying to go in the next 3–5 years, and why. Not a title — a *shape*. What alignment are you looking for? What would make the next role feel like progress?

---

## 9. Personal context

Background facts that aren't in the CV but color how you operate. Languages, formative experiences, how you got into the field. Only include what you're comfortable having a hiring manager see.

---

## 10. Anti-persona — what I'm not

The framings that feel like someone else describing you, not you. Corporate tech-bro phrases ("rockstar", "10x", "disruptor", "passionate about synergies") that agents should never use in your voice. This is what prevents LLM drift into generic voice.
